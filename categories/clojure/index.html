Sometimes Clojure is More Concise than Ruby

I find that 90% of the time, Clojure is more verbose than Ruby. Here’s one simple example where Clojure wins a conciseness battle: ;; Subtract two vectors in Clojure (def a [10 9 8]) (def b [1 2 3]) (map - a b) ;; (9 7 5) # Subtract two vectors in Ruby a = [10, 9, 8] b = [1, 2, 3] a.zip(b).map { |(a, b)| a - b } # [9, 7, 5] What is it about Ruby that makes it less concise in this example?
